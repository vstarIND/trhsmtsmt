When Abusing Parents Becomes “Cool”: A Classroom Reality No One Talks About

By The Rising Hindustan • February 2026

There is a silent culture growing inside many teenage classrooms — a culture where abusing someone’s parents is treated as humor.

It starts casually. A name taken loudly. A degrading remark. A burst of laughter. Soon, it becomes a pattern. A habit. A performance.

The louder the insult, the louder the applause. The more disrespectful the words, the more “cool” the speaker appears.

The Hidden Pain Behind the Laughter

But what about the student whose parents’ names are used as entertainment?

He smiles. Pretends it does not matter. But inside, something shifts.

Parents are not part of school rivalry. They are not part of social competition. Yet their names are dragged into jokes to gain dominance.

Over time, repeated humiliation affects confidence. It weakens focus. It builds silent anger.

Peer Pressure and Superiority

Teenage groups reward shock value. Disrespect becomes a shortcut to popularity.

Some students do it to show superiority. Some do it to avoid becoming the next target. Some follow because influential classmates normalize it.

Slowly, respect becomes weakness. And humiliation becomes entertainment.

The Emotional Consequences

Marks are recorded. Attendance is tracked. Performance is evaluated.

But emotional damage is not.

Students carry that humiliation home. They replay moments in silence. They question their worth. They feel unheard.

In extreme emotional states, constant humiliation combined with academic pressure can push vulnerable students toward harmful thoughts.

Real strength is not insulting someone’s parents. Real superiority is discipline and respect.

Classrooms should build character — not normalize emotional violence. Respect is not old-fashioned. It is foundational.
